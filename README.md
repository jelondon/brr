## Childcare and Benefits for Working Mothers in Islington

### Executive Summary

Islington offers working mothers a comprehensive support system combining government-funded childcare, tax benefits, income support, and local services. The key entitlements include up to 30 hours of free funded childcare weekly for infants from nine months, universal 15-hour entitlements for older preschoolers, Tax-Free Childcare providing up to £2,000 annually per child, and Child Benefit. Additional income support through Universal Credit and Council Tax Support schemes further reduces family costs. Eight council-managed nurseries operate locally alongside numerous private providers and childminders. Applications should begin at approximately 23 weeks of age, with formal applications made before specific annual deadlines tied to the child's ninth-month milestone.

***

### Government-Funded Childcare Entitlements

#### 30 Hours Free Childcare for Working Parents

The most significant entitlement for employed mothers is the 30 hours per week of free childcare available from the term after a child turns nine months old through until school entry. This represents 1,140 hours annually, "stretched" across 38 weeks per year, though eligible providers may extend hours across the full year to reduce the weekly commitment.[1]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

Working parents qualify if:[2][3][1]
- Both parents are in paid employment (if in a partnership), or the sole parent is working
- Each parent (or the sole parent) earns between £1,976 and £100,000 adjusted net income annually
- The child usually lives with them
- They live and work in England

Parents returning from maternity, paternity, shared parental, or adoption leave remain eligible provided they return or will return to work within one month of the term start date (30 September, 31 January, or 30 April). Self-employed parents qualify, including those in their first year of business with lower earnings thresholds, and parents on zero-hour contracts are eligible if they have arranged childcare for their available hours.[4][2]

**Application Timeline:**

Applications open 23 weeks before the child's ninth month and close according to a strict calendar:[3][2]

| Child's 9-Month Milestone | Access From | Application Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 January to 31 March | 1 April term | 31 March |
| 1 April to 31 August | 1 September term | 31 August |
| 1 September to 31 December | 1 January term | 31 December |

Applying early is recommended, with most authorities suggesting submission at least six weeks before the deadline, as some providers request codes earlier.[3]

Once approved, parents receive a unique working parents code from HMRC for each child, which they present to their chosen provider. Reconfirmation of eligibility is required every three months via the HMRC online account. If circumstances change and parents become ineligible, a grace period applies, allowing continued access for a short period before the funding ends.[1]

#### Universal 15-Hour Entitlement

All three and four-year-olds are entitled to 15 hours per week of free early education (570 hours annually). This is not means-tested and does not require employment. Eligible 2-year-olds from qualifying backgrounds (certain benefits recipients with household income under £15,400 annually) can also access 15 hours.[5][1]

### Tax and Income Benefits for Childcare Costs

#### Tax-Free Childcare

Tax-Free Childcare provides a government top-up of childcare payments, independent of the free childcare hours. The scheme operates on a deposit system where parents contribute to an online account and the government adds a matching amount.[6][7][8]

**How It Works:**
- For every 80 pence parents deposit, the government adds 20 pence
- Maximum government contribution: £500 every three months (£2,000 annually per child)
- For disabled children: £1,000 every three months (£4,000 annually)
- Maximum childcare costs eligible: £10,000 per child annually[7]

**Eligibility:**
- Parents aged 16 or older working (including self-employed)
- Both parents must work if partnered, unless one receives disability benefits (Incapacity Benefit, Severe Disablement Allowance, Carer's Allowance, Limited Capability for Work Benefit, or contribution-based Employment and Support Allowance)
- Child must be 11 or younger (17 if disabled)
- Cannot be claimed alongside the childcare element of Universal Credit[8]

**Compatible With Other Support:**
Tax-Free Childcare can be combined with the 30 hours free childcare, making it valuable for covering costs beyond government-funded hours or for gap-filling during school holidays. The scheme is available for registered childcare only—nurseries, childminders, nannies, after-school clubs, and holiday schemes all qualify.[7][8]

#### Child Benefit

Child Benefit provides a non-means-tested payment to all qualifying families regardless of income or savings.[9][10]

**Current Rates (2025/26):**
- £26.05 per week for the first child
- £17.25 per week for each additional child
- Annual payment for one child: £1,354.60

**High Income Child Benefit Charge:**

A significant consideration for higher earners is the clawback mechanism. From April 2024, the threshold is £60,000 individual adjusted net income. Above this threshold:[10][11][9]
- For every £200 earned over £60,000, parents must repay 1% of the Child Benefit
- At £80,000 income and above, the entire benefit is clawed back through tax[12][9]

However, it remains worthwhile to claim even when subject to clawback, as the applicant receives National Insurance credits that count toward state pension entitlement.[9]

#### Universal Credit Childcare Element

Low-income working families may qualify for the childcare element of Universal Credit, which contributes towards registered childcare costs.[13][14]

**Maximum Awards (2025/26):**
- £1,031.88 per month for one child
- £1,768.94 per month for two or more children

This represents up to 85% of actual childcare costs, whichever is lower.[14][15][13]

**Eligibility:**
- Parent must be in paid work (including self-employment) or have a firm job offer starting within the next assessment period
- Childcare must be with registered providers (OFSTED registered in England)
- Covers all children until 31 August after their 16th birthday
- Cannot be claimed simultaneously with Tax-Free Childcare[14]

Universal Credit is asset-tested (under £16,000 in savings between household members) and income-tested. Parents transitioning back to work may benefit, as childcare costs for a "settling-in period" before employment begins and during the first assessment period after job loss are covered.[14]

### Islington-Specific Childcare Provision

#### Council-Managed Nurseries

Islington operates eight directly-managed nurseries where childcare fees are subsidised based on household income, the number of hours required, the child's age, and residency status.[16][17]

**How to Register:**

There is no centralised admissions process. Parents apply directly to their preferred nursery by completing a registration form. The form requires:[17]
- Parent name, email, phone, address
- Employment/study status
- Child's date of birth
- Days required and desired start date

Following registration, the council sends an admissions form. All admissions forms join a waiting list, and spaces are allocated throughout the year as they become available. Parents are welcome to book nursery tours before applying.[17]

#### Private Nurseries and Childminders

Beyond council-managed provision, Islington hosts numerous private registered nurseries, pre-schools, childminders, and holiday clubs. Providers include establishments rated "Outstanding" and "Good" by Ofsted. The Islington Council website provides searchable directories of registered childcare providers in each area.[18][19]

#### Bright Start Services

Islington's integrated support service for families with children under five provides free services beyond childcare:[20]

- Health visiting teams across North, Central, and South areas
- New Parents Groups for social connection and topic-based learning
- Parenting programmes and courses
- Breastfeeding support and feeding guidance
- Parental mental wellbeing support
- Family Hubs serving as access points for services for children 0-19 (up to 25 with special educational needs)
- Library services for families
- Support for children with special educational needs and disabilities
- Child health clinics

Registration for Bright Start is free and can be done by completing a registration form, allowing access to drop-in sessions, activities, and targeted support.[20]

#### Home-Start Camden & Islington

For families experiencing significant stress or isolation, Home-Start offers free, confidential support through trained volunteers who provide emotional and practical help. The service specifically supports families with children under five.[21]

#### Family Information Service (FIS)

Islington's award-winning FIS provides information and advice for families with children aged 0-25. Contact details:[5][20]
- **Phone:** 020 7527 5959
- **Email:** FIS@islington.gov.uk

The FIS can assist with locating childcare, understanding entitlements, and navigating support services.

### Income and Other Qualifying Support

#### Council Tax Support Scheme

Islington maintains one of the most generous Council Tax Support schemes nationally. For working-age claimants:[22][23][24]
- Up to 95% of council tax is covered
- For the most financially vulnerable, 100% support is provided (no council tax payment required)
- Approximately £28 million is allocated annually to this scheme

This scheme provides substantial relief for low and middle-income families regardless of whether they claim other benefits.[22]

#### Household Support Fund

Additionally, eligible families may access the Household Support Fund, which provides £200 per sick or disabled child in the household. Qualifying families must also be receiving a means-tested benefit (Housing Benefit, Council Tax Support, Universal Credit, Pension Credit, or income-related Employment and Support Allowance).[25]

#### Income Maximisation Service

Islington Council operates an Income Maximisation Service to help residents identify all available benefits and support. Since April 2024, the service has helped secure £16.2 million in additional income for residents. This can be valuable for understanding and maximising available entitlements.[22]

### Application Timeline and Practical Steps

#### When to Begin

**Newborn to 23 Weeks:**
- Register with Bright Start for free family support and health visiting services
- Contact the Family Information Service to discuss childcare options
- Book visits to potential childcare providers

**At 23 Weeks of Age:**
- Begin applications for the 30-hour working parents code (if eligible)
- Register with preferred council-managed nurseries
- Apply to private nurseries and childminders as preferred

#### Key Application Dates

**For 30-Hour Free Childcare:**

The application deadline depends on when the child turns nine months:

- **Child reaches 9 months 1 Jan–31 Mar:** Apply by 31 March to access from April term
- **Child reaches 9 months 1 Apr–31 Aug:** Apply by 31 August to access from September term  
- **Child reaches 9 months 1 Sept–31 Dec:** Apply by 31 December to access from January term

Applications are made online via the government's Childcare Service, requiring the National Insurance number (or UTR if self-employed), child's UK birth certificate reference (if available), employment start date, and information about any government support claimed.[26][3]

**For Council-Managed Nurseries:**

Once registered, admission forms are processed on a rolling basis throughout the year. However, most places become available for the autumn term, making early application advantageous.

**For School Nursery Classes:**

Application timelines vary by school. As an example, Yerbury Primary School accepts applications from when a child is two years old, with formal application deadlines around March for autumn term entry and July for spring term entry.[27]

#### Tax-Free Childcare

Once the 30-hour code has been received and validated with a provider, parents can separately apply for Tax-Free Childcare if eligible. This requires opening an online childcare account on the government website, adding childcare providers as payees, and depositing funds. Reconfirmation is required every three months.

### Income and Disqualifying Criteria Summary

| Support Scheme | Income Limits | Key Disqualifying Factors |
|---|---|---|
| 30-Hour Free Childcare | £1,976–£100,000 adjusted net income | Either parent earning £100,000+; child doesn't live with parents; neither parent working |
| Tax-Free Childcare | £1,976–£100,000 (if both in couples; earnings via single employment) | Claiming childcare element of UC; child over 12 (17 if disabled); claimed simultaneously with UC childcare element |
| Child Benefit | None (not means-tested) | Child age 16+ (20 if in education); not UK resident; Immigration status requirements |
| Universal Credit (childcare element) | Means-tested; under £16,000 savings | Not in paid work; claiming Tax-Free Childcare; childcare not registered; child 16+ (17 with disability) |
| Council Tax Support | Highly generous—up to 100% for vulnerable households | Generally available to all low-income residents |

### Recommended Action Steps for a Working Mother

1. **Immediately (upon discovery of pregnancy or after birth):** Register with Bright Start for family support and health visiting services.

2. **By 16 weeks before the child's ninth month:** Contact FIS (020 7527 5959) to discuss options; begin visiting potential nurseries.

3. **At 23 weeks of age:** 
   - Register with preferred council-managed nurseries
   - Apply to private nurseries and childminders
   - Begin gathering documents for the 30-hour code application (National Insurance number, birth certificate reference, employment dates)

4. **By the application deadline** (31 March, 31 August, or 31 December, depending on ninth-month date):
   - Submit 30-hour code application via the government Childcare Service
   - Confirm applications with all nurseries and providers

5. **Upon receiving the 30-hour code:** Present it to the childcare provider to activate funding.

6. **If eligible:** Apply for Tax-Free Childcare and open the online account to begin receiving government top-ups.

7. **Ongoing:** Reconfirm eligibility with HMRC every three months for the 30-hour code and Tax-Free Childcare; maintain communication with childcare providers regarding funded hours availability.

### Conclusion

Islington offers substantial support for working mothers through a combination of generous government-funded childcare entitlements, tax benefits, and targeted local services. The 30-hour free childcare from nine months is particularly valuable, reducing out-of-pocket costs significantly. Combined with Tax-Free Childcare for any additional hours, Child Benefit, and means-tested support through Universal Credit or Council Tax Support for eligible families, the cumulative support can substantially offset childcare costs. Early engagement with local services, timely application submission, and ongoing reconfirmation of eligibility are essential to maximising available support. The Islington Family Information Service remains the primary point of contact for tailored advice specific to individual family circumstances.
